Poetry Gallery By: Alexandria Vasquez

As a poet, every feeling, emotion, experience, person, and place is unique and deserves to be celebrated through the art of poetry. This is why my collection consists of 100 poems that each represent something different, a testament to the diversity of human experiences. From love and heartbreak to joy and sorrow, my poems explore the full spectrum of emotions. One poem in my collection is dedicated to the feeling of nostalgia. It captures those moments when we long for things from our memories that are bittersweet but still hold a special place in our hearts. Another poem explores the complexities of grief and mourning, how we navigate loss and find meaning in the face of tragedy. Each poem paints a vivid picture with its words, taking readers on an emotional journey that touches on various aspects of what it means to be human.

What Could’ve Been Time is something I take for granted I spend it thinking about what could’ve been Instead of focusing on what happened Why didn’t it work out the way I wanted? Is it me? Am I the problem? There are things I will never understand What happens beyond my control should not be my concern What goes through someone else's mind I will never know Obsessing over an idea that never happened Wishing it happened I wonder if you think about what could’ve been -AFV
Our Planet God’s creation The planet people take for granted Looking at pictures of views around the world Going to those places and experiencing them in real time Views allow a person to see the world from a new lens All we need is a new lens from time to time -AFV
Wishing Get out of my mind I beg to be released From the fake scenarios All these wishes surrounding me I keep thinking i’ve moved on Nothing ever happened But why do I keep you in my mind? Why are you so important?    - AFV
I’m Never The One When will it be my turn? I wait in vain, patiently Waiting for my turn to be seen I try and try, again and again, over and over Almost like thrashing through water when drowning I’m drowning Drowning in my attempts When will you finally see me? -AFV
